Spatial and Phylogenetic Patterns Reveal Hidden Infection Sources of Bacillus anthracis in an Anthrax Outbreak in Son La Province, Vietnam

University of Florida | Emerging Pathogens Institute | Published 2023 | Infection, Genetics and Evolution

Overview

In April 2022, an anthrax outbreak in Son La Province, Vietnam exposed 137 people across three villages following the butchering of a water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis). Early investigations suggested a single animal was the source of all human exposures. By combining whole genome sequencing with spatial analysis, this study challenged that assumption — revealing unexpected genetic diversity among isolates that pointed to multiple, likely undetected, animal sources circulating in the region.

This work demonstrates how integrating molecular epidemiology with place-based spatial reasoning can uncover hidden transmission dynamics that traditional outbreak investigations miss.

Methods

  1. EightB. anthracis isolates were recovered from human clinical cases, buffalo hide, associated maggots, and soil at the carcass site.
  2. Whole genome sequencing performed on all isolates, enabling global, regional, and local molecular epidemiological analyses.
  3. Canonical single nucleotide polymorphism (canSNP) analysis used to assign isolates to global lineage and contextualize within regional strains from China, India, Indonesia, Thailand, and Australia.
  4. Twenty-five marker multi-locus variable number tandem repeat analysis (MLVA-25) applied to assess fine-scale genetic relationships among human, animal, and environmental isolates.
  5. Spatial analysis conducted to map case locations, isolate recovery sites, and village-level exposure patterns across the three affected communities.
  6. Coupled spatial and phylogenetic findings interpreted alongside local news reports and field epidemiological data to reconstruct outbreak dynamics.

Key Findings

  • Multiple hidden sources, not one. Four distinct MLVA-25 genotypes were identified from just eight isolates — an unusually high level of genetic diversity for an outbreak of this geographic scale and timeframe, directly contradicting the initial single-source hypothesis.
  • A lineage new to Vietnam. All isolates belonged to the A.Br.001/002 lineage, which had not previously been identified in Vietnam. Its presence in neighboring China, India, Indonesia, and Thailand suggests regional circulation that has gone undetected.
  • Spatial patterns supported the genomic signal. The geographic distribution of cases across three villages, combined with phylogenetic clustering, pointed to independent exposure events rather than a single propagating chain — a finding later corroborated by local reports of at least two additional buffalo deaths.
  • Outbreak response must include intensive animal surveillance. These findings underscore that human anthrax investigations cannot be limited to the index animal. Undetected livestock deaths in the surrounding area represent ongoing and unquantified exposure risks.
